### Otōshi (お通し) — Table Charge
When you sit down, you will receive a small appetizer called **"Otōshi"**.

- This is a standard custom at Japanese izakayas
- It serves as a table/seating charge (typically ¥500–¥800 per person)
- It is **not optional** — this is normal in Japan
- Think of it as a cover charge that comes with a complimentary small dish

## 🍽️ DURING YOUR MEAL

### How to Order
1. Your server will bring you an **Oshibori** (hot towel) — use it to clean your hands
2. Start with drinks — say **"Toriaezu, biiru"** (Beer first!) or ask for sake recommendations
3. Order dishes gradually — izakaya dining is meant to be leisurely
4. Dishes are meant to be **shared** among the table
5. Feel free to order more throughout the evening

### Useful Japanese Phrases

| English | Japanese | Pronunciation |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| "Excuse me" (to call staff) | すみません | *Sumimasen* |
| "I'd like to order" | 注文お願いします | *Chuumon onegaishimasu* |
| "What do you recommend?" | おすすめは何ですか？ | *Osusume wa nan desu ka?* |
| "Chef's choice, please" | おまかせでお願いします | *Omakase de onegaishimasu* |
| "This is delicious!" | おいしい！ | *Oishii!* |
| "One more, please" | もう一つお願いします | *Mou hitotsu onegaishimasu* |
| "I'm allergic to ___" | ___アレルギーがあります | *___ arerugii ga arimasu* |
| "No raw fish, please" | 生魚はなしでお願いします | *Nama-zakana wa nashi de onegaishimasu* |
| "Check, please" | お会計お願いします | *Okaikei onegaishimasu* |

### Dining Etiquette Tips
- **Chopsticks**: Never stick them upright in rice (this resembles a funeral ritual)
- **Sake**: It is polite to pour for others, and let others pour for you
- **Noise**: Slurping noodles and soup is perfectly acceptable — even encouraged!
- **Shoes**: Keep your shoes on (no shoe removal required at this restaurant)

## 💴 PAYMENT — お会計

### How to Pay

1. When you're ready to leave, say: **"Okaikei onegaishimasu"** (お会計お願いします)
2. Your server will bring the bill to your table, or direct you to the register near the entrance
3. **Review your bill** — it will include:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Food & Drinks | Everything you ordered |
| Otōshi | Table charge (per person) |
| Tax | Already included in listed prices (税込) |
| Service Charge | May apply for private rooms |

### Payment Methods

| Method | Accepted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| 💴 **Cash (JPY)** | ✅ Yes | Most reliable method |
| 💳 **Credit Card** | ✅ Yes | Visa, Mastercard, etc. |
| 📱 **QR Code Payment** | Please ask | PayPay, etc. |
| 💵 **Foreign Currency** | ❌ No | Please use Japanese Yen only |

> 💡 *We recommend confirming accepted payment methods when making your reservation or upon arrival.*

### Important Notes About Payment in Japan

- **No tipping** — Tipping is not customary in Japan. Excellent service is the standard, and tipping may cause confusion.
- **Pay at the register** — In many izakayas, you pay at the front register, not at the table.
- **One bill** — The bill is typically for the entire table. Splitting bills per person is generally not available. Please settle among yourselves afterward.
- **Receipts** — A receipt (レシート *reshiito*) will be provided. Ask **"Ryōshūsho onegaishimasu"** (領収書お願いします) if you need a formal receipt for business purposes.
